PCPC Communications Style Guide

As you write for PCPC publications, the website, or any print material with group distribution, please follow these guidelines for clarity and consistency. Our identity is a visual representation of our church and, more importantly, our Lord, and our desire is that we reflect His character in its cohesiveness.

For anything not covered in this document, please refer to Merriam-Webster’s Collegiate Dictionary and The Chicago Manual of Style. Some of the following guidelines are exceptions to those reference authorities; others address frequently asked questions here at PCPC.

Numerals

Telephone numbers: No parentheses or dots; don’t precede with at:

RSVP to G-Money, 214-224-2555.

Times: Always include two digits after the colon. Always include a space between the numbers and the letters. No periods and no caps on am and pm.

7:00 am
10:35 pm

Dates: Always include commas around years. No ths, nds, or sts.

April 25, 2004 September, 1939

Numerals: Spell out all numbers nine and less.

We bagged 94 of them doves.
I don’t own but two guns.

Percentages:

40%

Money:

$5
$5.25

Capitalization

E-mail addresses (note the hyphen in e-mail) are uncapitalized:

bilbo.baggins@pcpc.org

Capitalize locations in buildings:

Room 301
Chapel
Parlor
Call Center
Oak Lawn West

Capitalize the names of ministries:

Women’s Ministries
60+ Ministries

Capitalize individual professional titles:

Kari Stainback, Director of Women’s Ministries

Lowercase general professional titles:

Could all of the pastors fit in one elevator?

Don’t bold or underline anything, except dates and times of upcoming events.

Lepidopterists for Christ will meet Thursday, April 16, 5:40 pm.

Capitalize Kingdom, Bible, Body, Gospel, Good News, Great Commission, Protestant, Capital Campaign, Redeemer, Advent, Communion, Reformed, Resurrection (the event)

Don’t capitalize scriptures, church, biblical, gospel, crucifixion, creation, cross

Capitalize pronouns referring to the Trinity: He, His, Him, You, Your, My, Mine.

Community is capitalized in conjunction with a particular Sunday school name, but lowercase in general:

The Veritas Community meets regularly.
We’re so grateful for this community

Church-Related

In general, do not include professional titles before names:

Mark Davis likes pie.
She spoke to Steve Vanderhill after class.

Refer to the different areas of PCPC as ministries, not departments.

Refer to Sunday school classes as communities, not classes.

Always use the address 4124 Oak Lawn and include website in printed material.

Punctuation

A comma follows the second-to-last item in a series:

Bring a salad, bread, or dessert.

Use a period after the cardinal direction in a street address:

The scene at 6000 W. Mockingbird was promising for evangelism.

No comma after short introductory adverbial phrase:

Once a month the ladies play golf.

Usage

Use first name only after first reference:

Mike Mingelhoffer told the men about his upcoming transition. Then Mike thanked them for their prayers.

Acronyms: Use full name with abbreviation in parentheses, then abbreviation after first reference:

Vacation Bible School (VBS) will be June 15–18.
Come to VBS for boatloads of fun!

The wife’s name comes first when referring to a married couple:

Kathy and John Jackson looked smashing in that wedding photo.

Style

Use quotation marks around the titles of radio programs:

I listen to “Transforming Presence” whenever I can.
